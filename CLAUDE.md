## Project Overview

Road networks are reshaping Greater Kumasi as the primary driver of landscape transformation. The study uses multi-temporal satellite imagery (Landsat) to compare land cover across decades, classifying terrain into: **forests, settlements, water bodies, farmlands/agricultural land**. Change detection techniques pinpoint how much natural greenery has been converted by urban expansion. Buffer zone analysis around major highways reveals how development intensity varies with distance from transport corridors.

**Core finding pattern**: Buildings and businesses aggressively cluster along new transport corridors → forests are fragmented → Kumasi loses its "Garden City" status → impervious surfaces trap heat (Urban Heat Island effect) → loss of wetlands worsens seasonal flash flooding.

## Research Objectives

1. Map and quantify the spatial-temporal dynamics of LULC changes in Greater Kumasi over **1995–2024**
2. Evaluate how proximity to major transport corridors accelerates the loss of agricultural livelihoods and ecological zones
